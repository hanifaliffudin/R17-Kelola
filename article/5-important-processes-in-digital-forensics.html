5 Important Processes in Digital Forensics

In this era of digitization, most digital activities have been recorded on the internet and various digital devices. The entire recording is a digital footprint and can be an alternative to prove a criminal act in court. This is known as Digital Forensics, a branch of forensic science that uses data on criminal acts.

Digital forensics is used to search for evidence, confirm the truth, and determine and identify sources of information. There are five essential processes when doing Digital Forensics. What is this critical process like?

  • Identification

    • Identification is a significant first step in the forensic examination process. This directly impacts the subsequent process and the outcome of the investigative project. This step usually includes gathering sources of electronic evidence or digital information relevant to the investigation being conducted.

  • Preservation

    • The next step is to select sources to ensure that electronic evidence stored on ESI (Electronically Stored Information) devices is not tampered with, altered, or even removed, which can complicate the investigation process.

  • Collection

    • The process of gathering digital information that may be relevant and helpful in the investment process. This collection can take the form of removing the ESI device from the scene.

  • Analysis

    • This stage is carried out to draw conclusions based on the evidence found by searching for evidence on the ESI device. The results of this process include data objects that have been analyzed based on investigations carried out using digital forensic analysis methods.

  • Reporting

    • The final process when the investigation has been completed is preparing a report based on proven techniques and methodologies. This report contains case data, examination objectives, results, conclusions, and documentation of investigative evidence presented in detail.
